About Benjamin.




Benjamin’s decision to become a sommelier originated with the idea to encompass each of his core passions: food, travel, people, and nature! Driven by his commitment towards wine and his desire to learn more about the industry, Benjamin has thrived on traveling the world. During his time in New York City, he took trips to explore the renowned wine regions of France and Italy. Through his travels, Benjamin gained valuable firsthand knowledge and expertise of wine and European culture while connecting with winemakers. Wine is the focal point of Benjamin’s aspirations because, let’s face it - without wine, dining and drinking would be far less entertaining!

Benjamin grew up in the equestrian-centered village of Wellington, Florida, immediately jump-starting his career in the hospitality industry through the Fashion Cuisine Group. Over three years, he assisted with the openings of three restaurants, advancing from server until his eventual exit from the company as manager. Benjamin’s time as captain at Table 26 - with its fine wine and elevated dining - allowed for him to focus on a career in the fine dining hospitality field.

Benjamin swiftly moved to New York City, where he had the privilege of working with classic French chefs and world-class wine lists. He achieved his certified sommelier degree in November 2017. One year after working at the renowned Cafe Boulud, known for its fine French dining, under Chef Daniel Boulud, he accepted a sommelier position from Chef Gabriel Kreuther. During Benjamin’s two years as a sommelier and manager at Restaurant Gabriel Kreuther, the team achieved two-stars Michelin, Les Grandes Tables du Monde, and the AAA 5 Diamond award. They were also a member of the Relais & Chateaux family. Benjamin was then recruited by Gurney’s Montauk to oversee and maintain the Scarpetta Beach Wine program in Montauk, New York, for the summer season of 2018.

Benjamin had ambitious plans to spend the summer of 2020 in France working harvest in the vineyards of Burgundy. However, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, he deviated from his plan and instead took an opportunity at Cain Vineyard and Winery in Napa Valley, California. After the 2020 harvest, he returned to Wellington to launch Wellington Wine Consulting. Benjamin looks forward to bringing his wealth of knowledge and worldwide cultural experiences to his clientele in south Florida!
